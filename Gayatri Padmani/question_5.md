Question 1 : What are the common types of features in a dataset? Explain categorical, numerical, and ordinal features.

Answer: 

The “features” are the pieces of information we use to make predictions. These features usually fall into a few common types:

1. Numerical Features:
 - These are numbers that you can do math with — like age, height, salary, or temperature.

 - Example: Age = 25, Salary = 50,000.

 - You can add, subtract, take averages, etc.

2. Categorical Features:

 - These are categories or labels — they describe qualities rather than numbers.

 - Example: Color = Red, Green, Blue or City = Mumbai, Delhi, Bangalore.

 - You can’t really do math on them, but they help the model understand groups.

3. Ordinal Features:

 - These are a mix — they’re categories with an order. The difference between the values isn’t always the same, but the order matters.

 - Example: Education = High School < Bachelor < Master < PhD or Rating = Poor < Good < Excellent.

 - The model knows one is “higher” or “better” than the other, even if we don’t know the exact difference.
